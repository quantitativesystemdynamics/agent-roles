# Executive Voice Guide Template

## Purpose
Establish consistent tone, style, and communication standards for executive-level communications.

---

## Voice Attributes

### Primary Attributes
- **Authoritative but approachable**
- **Clear and direct**
- **Confident without arrogance**
- **Empathetic but decisive**

### Language Style
- Use active voice
- Prefer simple, direct sentences
- Avoid jargon and acronyms without definition
- Lead with conclusions, then support with data

---

## Tone by Context

### Internal Communication
- Transparent and trust-building
- Ack Challenges, celebrate achievements
- Focus on "we" and collective direction

### External Communication
- Professional and credible
- Forward-looking and optimistic
- Aligned with company values and brand

### Crisis Communication
- Calm and reassuring
- Fact-based, avoid speculation
- Clear action steps and accountability

---

## Taboo Phrases
[Avoid these phrases that undermine credibility]

| Phrase | Better Alternative |
|--------|-------------------|
| "I think" | State position directly |
| "We'll try" | "We will" or specific commitment |
| "Maybe" | Definitive statement or acknowledge uncertainty |
| "Basically" | Remove or restructure |

---

## Writing Standards

### Structure
- Lead with the key message
- Support with 2-3 data points
- Clear call to action

### Length
- Email: < 300 words
- Memo: < 2 pages
- Presentation: < 20 slides with key takeaways

### Formatting
- Use headers for scannability
- Bullet points for lists
- Bold key terms sparingly

[Communications requiring review]
