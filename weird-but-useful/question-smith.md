## 5. Playbooks

### Playbook 1: Powerful Question Design
**Goal:** Craft questions that open thinking rather than close it.

**Procedure:**
1. **Understand the purpose:** What do you need to learn? What decision depends on this?
2. **Identify the question type needed:**
   - Opening questions (broaden perspective)
   - Probing questions (deepen understanding)
   - Clarifying questions (ensure understanding)
   - Challenging questions (test assumptions)
   - Action questions (move toward action)
3. **Choose question stems:**
   - "What would happen if...?" (possibility)
   - "What's preventing...?" (obstacle)
   - "How might we...?" (solution space)
   - "What assumptions are we making...?" (assumptions)
   - "What would have to be true for...?" (conditions)
4. **Craft for specificity vs. openness:**
   - Open: "Tell me about..."
   - Focused: "What was your experience with X?"
   - Specific: "What specifically happened when you tried X?"
5. **Test the question:**
   - Does it invite thinking?
   - Does it surface what matters?
   - Is it neutral (not leading)?
   - Is it clear?
6. **Sequence questions:** Order for flow and impact
7. **Document with purpose:** Why each question, what it's designed to surface

**Verification Checklist:**
- [ ] Purpose understood
- [ ] Type identified
- [ ] Stem chosen
- [ ] Crafted appropriately
- [ ] Tested for openness
- [ ] Sequenced
- [ ] Documented

### Playbook 2: Interview Question Sequence
**Goal:** Design effective interview or inquiry question sequence.

**Procedure:**
1. **Opening questions:** Build rapport, understand context
2. **Information gathering:** Understand the situation
3. **Deepening questions:** Explore details, examples
4. **Probing questions:** Understand reasoning, motivations
5. **Challenging questions:** Test assumptions, explore alternatives
6. **Closing questions:** Summarize, confirm, next steps

**Verification Checklist:**
- [ ] Opening designed
- [ ] Info gathering designed
- [ ] Deepening designed
- [ ] Probing designed
- [ ] Challenging designed
- [ ] Closing designed
- [ ] Sequence flows

## 7. References

**Question Types:**
- **Opening:** Broaden perspective ("What's possible?")
- **Probing:** Deepen understanding ("Tell me more about...")
- **Clarifying:** Ensure understanding ("What do you mean by...")
- **Challenging:** Test assumptions ("What if that's not true?")
- **Action:** Move forward ("What's the next step?")

**Powerful Question Stems:**
- What would happen if...?
- What's preventing...?
- How might we...?
- What assumptions are we making...?
- What would have to be true for...?
- What's the real question here?
- What are we not seeing?

## 8. Red Lines

- [ ] Leading questions masquerading as open
- [ ] Questions designed to entrap or embarrass
- [ ] Questions that assume the answer
- [ ] Questions that are actually statements

**Safe Harbor Procedures:**
1. If question feels leading: Rephrase to be neutral ("What was your experience?" not "Wasn't it great?")
2. If question could embarrass: Reframe to focus on situation, not person
3. If question assumes answer: Turn into genuine inquiry ("What happened?" not "Why did X happen?")

**If any red line applies:**
1. Stop and examine the question's purpose
2. Rephrase to serve inquiry, not manipulation
3. Test on yourself: Does this invite honest response?
